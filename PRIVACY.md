# Privacy Policy — Xero for Gmail

**Last updated:** March 2026

Xero for Gmail (“the extension”) is a Chrome extension that shows Xero contact and financial data in a Gmail side panel. This policy describes what data the extension uses and where it goes.

## Data the extension uses

- **Gmail sender address**  
  When you open the side panel on a Gmail thread, the extension reads the sender email of the current conversation. This is used only to look up the corresponding contact and their invoices, bills, purchase orders, and quotes in Xero. The email is not sent to our servers or stored by us.

- **Xero and Google sign-in**  
  The extension uses OAuth to access your Xero account and (for Gmail) your Google account. OAuth tokens and your selected Xero organisation are stored locally in your browser via Chrome’s storage. We do not receive or store your passwords or tokens on our own systems.

- **Usage analytics**  
  The extension sends usage events to Google Analytics (GA4), such as “contact found,” “link opened,” and similar actions. A random installation ID (client_id) is stored locally and sent with these events to count unique users. No email addresses, names, or other personally identifiable information are sent to analytics.

## Where data is sent

- **Your browser only** — OAuth tokens and preferences stay in Chrome storage on your device.
- **Xero** — Contact and financial data is requested from Xero’s API when you use the extension.
- **Google** — Gmail sender is read via Google’s APIs; usage events are sent to Google Analytics as described above. We do not sell or share this data with third parties for advertising or other unrelated purposes.

## Your choices

- You can uninstall the extension at any time; local data in the extension’s storage is removed when you remove the extension.

## Compliance

- We do not sell or transfer user data to third parties for purposes unrelated to the extension’s single purpose.
- We do not use or transfer user data for creditworthiness or lending purposes.

## Contact

For questions about this policy or the extension, open an issue in the project repository where the extension is developed.
